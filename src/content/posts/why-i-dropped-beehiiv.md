I started the newsletter on beehiiv because it was the fastest way to get
something in front of people. It did its job. But every post I wrote lived on
someone else's domain, in someone else's HTML, behind someone else's analytics.

So I'm moving writing back home. Posts now live here, on `pedrocastro.eu`, as
plain Markdown files in a git repo. There's an [RSS feed](/rss.xml). There's no
server, no database, and no third party between me and the words.

## What changes

- Posts are files. Publishing is `git push`.
- The URL of every post is dated — `/posts/2026/06/19/...` — which leaves room
  for year and month archives later.
- The feed is the subscription. If you read by RSS, nothing about your habit
  needs to change.

## What's next

This is the first of a few sections I'm building out: a media log of what I'm
listening to and reading, a feed of links worth keeping, and photos. All static,
all build-time, all mine. More soon.
